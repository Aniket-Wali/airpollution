A NATIONAL AIR QUALITY SERVICE PROVIDER

Information and data are prerequisites for an effective Air Quality Management . This means having information on :
  • what is the level of pollution (monitoring trends)
  • which areas are the most polluted (spatial trends)
  • who is contributing to this pollution (source trends)
  • when do pollution levels peak (temporal trends)
  • what are the options we have to reduce pollution (control trends).

The “Air Pollution Statistical Management” is designed to provide a starting point for understanding air pollution in cities in India. It brings together information from disparate sources to present a clear picture of the state of air pollution in that city.

This involves creating a detailed information database that includes pulling together data from disparate sources, surveys, mapping and atmospheric modeling. Deciding on policy options based on this information and implementing them effectively is the next step.

The analysis presents data extracted from a global database. The data is available at 0.1° and 0.01° resolution. We utilize the later to build these tables and charts, and also aggregated to state and city levels.

Air pollution is a serious environmental health issue in India. The global burden of disease assessments estimated 0.74 million and 1.1 million premature deaths in 1990 and 2016 due to outdoor PM2.5 and ozone pollution and 0.99 million and 0.78 million premature deaths in 1990 and 2016 due to household (indoor) PM2.5 pollution (GBD, 2018) .
